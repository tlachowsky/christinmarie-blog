Carmy Approved: The Walmart Pet Finds Our Dog Actually Uses

Affiliate disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through my links I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. See my full disclosure policy.

Let me tell you about Carmy.

Carmy is our dog. He is beloved, he is dramatic, he is fully convinced that every decision made in this household should be run by him first. He has a favorite spot on the couch (mine, specifically), a strong opinion about what time dinner should happen, and the ability to look profoundly betrayed by very minor inconveniences.

He is also, if I'm being honest, a little bit spoiled. This is entirely our fault. Three kids who thought he hung the moon from the day we brought him home will do that to a dog.

What I have learned in our years with Carmy: dogs have preferences, and buying things they won't use is a waste of money. I've made that mistake. I've bought the bed he ignored, the toy he was bored by in four minutes, the food bowl he pushed across the kitchen floor every meal because it wasn't weighted right. Lesson learned.

Here's what Carmy has actually approved. All from Walmart. All things I'd buy again.

The Bed He Actually Sleeps In

After two failed beds (one he ignored entirely, one he destroyed within a week), I found a simple bolster style bed at Walmart that he has slept in every single night since. He also sleeps on the couch. And my side of the bed when I get up in the morning. But the dog bed is home base and he uses it. That's a win.

The Food and Water Bowls

Weighted, non-slip. This sounds like a small thing but if you have a dog who slides their bowl across the floor during every meal you understand exactly why this matters. Carmy eats with enthusiasm and these bowls stay put. Transformed mealtime. Not exaggerating.

The Leash That Doesn't Tangle

We walk Carmy every evening as a family. It's one of those small routines that I love -- everyone's outside, nobody's looking at a screen, we're actually talking to each other. The leash situation matters when you walk daily. I've gone through a few. The one we have now from Walmart is exactly the right length, exactly the right weight, and doesn't tangle around legs. Essential.

The Grooming Basics

Carmy tolerates being groomed with approximately the same energy he brings to bath time, which is reluctant cooperation at best. We keep his grooming supplies in a basket by the back door -- brush, nail file, the shampoo he doesn't hate. All Walmart finds. The basket is labeled. Because of course it is.

The Treats

Carmy would like me to note that no amount of treats has ever been the right amount. He would also like more. We get his treats at Walmart because we go through them at a pace that requires reasonable pricing. He has no complaints about the quality. He has never had complaints about treats.

Final Carmy Note

He is currently asleep on the reading chair that is supposed to be mine. He looks extremely comfortable. I am choosing to be charmed by this rather than annoyed.

Dogs, man.

-- Christin Marie (and Carmy, who did not contribute to this post but would like credit anyway)
