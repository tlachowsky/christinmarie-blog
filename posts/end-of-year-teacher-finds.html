End of Year Teacher Life: What I'm Buying to Survive and Reset

Affiliate disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through my links I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. See my full disclosure policy.

May is a feeling. If you've never worked in a school, it's hard to fully explain -- it's the finish line and the last mile at the same time. Every teacher I know is simultaneously counting down the days and trying to hold everything together until they get there. It's a special kind of exhausted. It's also, if you love what you do, kind of wonderful.

This is my survival guide for May. What I buy, what I do, and how I set myself up so that August doesn't hit me like a freight train.

The Survive Part

Good coffee supplies. I'm being serious. The end of the year is not the time to let the coffee situation deteriorate. I have a small situation set up in my classroom for myself and I maintain it through the last day. Walmart has everything I need for this at prices that feel like self-care rather than a splurge.

Comfortable shoes. The last few weeks of school involve more standing, more moving, more everything than any other time of year. This is not the time for fashion footwear. I wear my most comfortable pair every day in May without apology.

Good pens for end of year paperwork. There is an amount of paperwork at the end of the school year that I will not describe in detail because it will only upset both of us. I have nice pens that I like writing with and they make it marginally better.

Student Appreciation

Every year I put together something small for my students at the end of the year. Nothing elaborate -- a little treat, a handwritten note if I can manage it, something that says I see you and this year mattered. I keep it budget-friendly because I'm doing this for an entire classroom and Walmart is where I make it work without spending more than I can afford.

Small treat bags, some candy, a few little school supplies for the summer. It comes together quickly and means more than you'd think.

The Reset Part

Before I leave my classroom for the summer I do a version of the deep clean I talked about in my fall classroom setup post, but in reverse. I assess what's left, what needs replacing, what I'm ordering over the summer so it arrives before August. I make my list. I note what worked and what didn't this year so I can adjust next year's setup.

This list lives in a notes app and I add to it all summer when I think of things. By August I have a complete picture of what I need and I'm not scrambling.

For Me, After the Last Day

A new book I've been saving. A long bath. A day where I do nothing school-related at all. And then I start thinking about next year, because I can't help it and I've stopped pretending otherwise.

To every teacher in the final stretch: you're almost there. You're doing something that matters. Rest this summer. You've earned it.

-- Christin Marie
