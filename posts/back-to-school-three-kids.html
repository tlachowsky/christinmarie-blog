Back to School With Three Teenagers: What We Actually Bought

I am both a mom sending three kids back to school and a teacher going back to school herself. This means I approach the back to school season with a very particular energy -- equal parts excitement, mild panic, and a detailed spreadsheet.

This year was a big one. E was a senior, which meant this was her last back to school at home. P started high school, which is a whole adjustment. A settled into her sophomore year like she'd always been there. Three kids, three completely different sets of needs.

What the Supply Lists Say vs. What You Actually Need

I've been a teacher long enough to be honest about this: school supply lists are not always a perfect reflection of what a child will actually use. Some things on the list will be essential. Some will sit in a backpack untouched until June. After many years of doing this, I've learned to read a list and make judgment calls.

What's always worth buying: good binders (the cheap ones fall apart by October), quality pens and pencils in bulk, a solid backpack that will last, and whatever the specific teacher has requested by name. If a teacher asks for a specific thing, get that specific thing. Trust me on this one.

What you can usually skip or buy later: excessive folders (one good system beats ten mediocre folders), themed supplies that cost more just for the design, and anything in quantities larger than what one child will realistically use in a year.

For E -- Her Last First Day

E is organized and always has been. She had opinions about what she needed and mostly she was right. We focused on quality basics -- a new planner she picked out herself, a few good pens she actually likes writing with, and some organizational supplies for her locker and her room. Nothing flashy. She's practical like that and I love it.

For P -- First Year of High School

P needed more help here. The jump from middle school to high school in terms of organizational demands is real and we talked through it. New backpack (his middle school one was genuinely held together by hope), a solid binder system, and his own label maker because I converted him to the label life early. He'll thank me later.

For A -- Hitting Her Stride

A knew exactly what she wanted and was very patient with me when I suggested alternatives. She's my most independent shopper of the three. She picked her own supplies, stayed under budget, and had opinions about every single thing. She is her mother's daughter.

The Walmart Strategy

We do all of our back to school shopping at Walmart and have for years. The selection is genuinely excellent, the prices don't require me to have a separate savings account for school supplies, and I can get everything in one trip -- which with three kids is not a small thing.

The best time to shop is mid-July through early August before the shelves get picked over, but after school lists have been released. Go early in the morning on a weekday if you can. That's the move.

The Total

Three kids, all supplies, under $300. That's with buying quality where it matters and being strategic everywhere else. Doable. Every year. At Walmart.

-- Christin Marie
