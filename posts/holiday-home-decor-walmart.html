Holiday Home on a Budget: My Walmart Christmas Haul

Christmas is my favorite time of year in this house. I know that's a common thing to say but I mean it in a very specific way -- I love the way our home feels when it's decorated for the holidays. The lights, the smell, the coziness of it all. I love all of it.

What I do not love is the credit card bill that follows when you let yourself go at the fancy home decor stores. I have done that exactly once. Once was enough.

For several years now I've done our entire holiday decor refresh at Walmart and I have zero complaints. The selection is genuinely good, the prices are reasonable, and what I've bought has held up year after year.

What I Replaced This Year

I don't buy all new decorations every year -- that's both wasteful and expensive. I assess what I have, retire what's worn out or that I've just gotten tired of looking at, and refresh strategically.

This year I replaced our front door wreath, which had seen better days, and added a few new throw pillows in holiday colors to swap onto the couch. I also got a new set of string lights for the mantle because the old ones had two dead sections and I'd been tolerating them for two Christmases. Done tolerating it.

The Tree Situation

We have an artificial tree that we've had for many years. I add new ornaments most years -- usually one or two that mean something (there's a Disney ornament on that tree for every trip we've taken) and sometimes a few simple fillers to fill in gaps. Walmart's ornament selection in December is extensive and the prices are excellent.

Carmy has knocked the tree over twice in his life. We now anchor it to the wall. He seemed briefly humbled by this development and then forgot about it entirely.

The Things That Make the Biggest Impact

In my experience, the things that make a home feel the most Christmasy are not the most expensive things. It's the lights -- specifically warm white lights on everything. Garland on the mantle. A candle that smells like Christmas. A throw blanket in a holiday color on the couch. These are all achievable at Walmart prices and they transform a space.

For the Kids' Rooms

E, P, and A all have some small holiday decorations in their rooms. A string of lights, a small decorative piece, something that makes their space feel like the holidays too. I let them pick their own within reason. Even teenagers like their space to feel festive -- they just won't always admit it.

The Total

My entire holiday refresh this year came in under $80. That includes the new wreath, the throw pillows, the string lights, a few new ornaments, and some candles. Our house looked beautiful. Our guests said so. No credit card regret.

That's the whole point.

Wishing you all the warmest holiday season. Thanks for reading along this year.

-- Christin Marie
