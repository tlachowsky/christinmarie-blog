Sending E to College: Every Dorm Essential We Found at Walmart

Affiliate disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through my links I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. See my full disclosure policy.

E graduated high school. She is going to college. I am so incredibly proud of her and I am also not handling this well at all.

What I have done instead of processing my feelings appropriately is channel everything into making sure she has exactly what she needs for her dorm room. This is how I show love. Organization and preparation are my love languages and this is a very important moment to deploy them.

We did the entire dorm shopping list at Walmart. I'm sharing everything here because the number of people who have asked me "where do you even start?" when it comes to dorm prep is significant and I have thoughts.

Start With the Dorm Dimensions

Before you buy anything, get the measurements of the dorm room and the specific bed size. College dorm beds are often extra long twin -- this is not the same as a regular twin and regular twin bedding will not fit properly. Many schools post room dimensions on their housing websites. Get this information first. It will save you a return trip.

Bedding -- The Most Important Purchase

Extra long twin sheets, a comfortable comforter, at least two sets of sheets so she can rotate without scrambling to do laundry, and a good pillow. Walmart has a solid selection of extra long twin bedding and the prices are significantly better than the specialty college bedding stores. E picked her own pattern. It's very her. I love it.

A mattress topper. Dorm mattresses are notoriously uncomfortable. A mattress topper makes a real difference in sleep quality and college students need sleep. This is not optional in my opinion.

The Bathroom Situation

If E is in a dorm with shared bathrooms she needs a shower caddy, flip flops for the shower, a good robe for walking to and from, and quick-dry towels that won't mildew between uses. All of this is available at Walmart. The shower caddy in particular -- get one with drainage holes, not a solid bottom. Trust me.

A good toiletry organizer for her counter or desk space. Small dorm bathrooms (even private ones) require maximum organization. Her mother's daughter has this handled.

The Study Space

A good desk lamp with actual adjustable brightness. Dorm lighting is not sufficient for studying. A lamp from Walmart, ideally with a warm and cool setting, makes a real difference. Also a comfortable chair cushion if the desk chair is the standard uncomfortable kind.

Storage for her desk -- small bins, drawer organizers, a cup for pens. The organizational instinct is strong in our family and E embraces it. Her desk will be the most organized one on the floor.

Under the Bed Storage

Dorm rooms are small. Under the bed space is valuable. Flat storage bins with lids that fit under a raised dorm bed are the move. Bed risers to create more of that under-bed space are also worth having. Walmart has both. We got a set of four flat storage bins and a set of risers and suddenly E has significantly more storage than the room appears to have.

The Small Things That Matter

A first aid kit. Pain reliever, cold medicine, bandages -- the basics. She needs these and she should not be making a sick day trip to find them. A small bin stocked with the essentials, ready to go.

A power strip with surge protector. Dorms have limited outlets and she has many things to charge. This is non-negotiable.

Laundry supplies and a laundry bag she can carry easily to the laundry room. A mesh bag that closes so things don't spill. Detergent pods because they're easier than liquid in a shared laundry space. Dryer sheets. Done.

Hangers -- more than you think she'll need. Dorm closets are small but vertical space exists and good hangers help you use it.

The Part I Wasn't Ready For

We did all this shopping together. Just E and me, one Saturday, going through the list. She was patient with my opinions about shower caddies and let me add the first aid kit without argument. At the end she said "thanks Mom, this was actually really fun" and I almost cried in the Walmart home goods aisle.

She's going to be great. I know she is. And she's going to be great with excellent dorm room organization, which is all I can really do at this point.
