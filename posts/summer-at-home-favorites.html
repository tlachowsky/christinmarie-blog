Summer at Home: Our Favorite Backyard and Indoor Finds This Season

Affiliate disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through my links I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. See my full disclosure policy.

People always seem surprised when I tell them we don't really do big summer vacations. We do Disney when we can, but for regular old summer? We are home people. We love being home. We are very, very good at being home.

With three teenagers, summer at our house means late mornings, backyard time, movie nights, and a whole lot of just being together without a schedule. I love it. And over the years I've figured out exactly what makes summer at home feel like the best place to be.

Here's what we leaned on this summer.

For the Backyard

We are not fancy backyard people. We don't have a pool. We have a patio, some chairs, a grill, and the determination to be outside as much as Arkansas summers allow (which, if you've never experienced Arkansas in July, is less than you'd think -- the humidity is its own creature).

What actually gets used: a good set of outdoor chairs that don't mildew or fade, a patio umbrella that actually stays put in the wind, and an outdoor rug that makes the whole space feel more like a room. All of ours came from Walmart and have held up through multiple Arkansas summers, which is the real test.

Carmy is outside with us whenever we are. He takes his backyard supervision duties very seriously. We got him a nice outdoor water bowl this summer that doesn't tip over -- a small thing that made a big difference. He approved.

For Movie Nights

Friday night is movie night at our house and has been for years. Even now that the kids are teenagers they still show up for it, which means everything to me. We rotate who picks and nobody is allowed to complain about anyone else's choice. Those are the rules.

The things that make movie night work: good blankets, good snacks, and a couch that fits everyone. We have a blanket situation at our house that has gotten slightly out of control and I am not sorry about it. Soft throw blankets from Walmart are genuinely some of the best I've ever used, and at the price point they're at, you can just get several.

For the Kids

E spent most of her last summer before college at home, which I am selfishly grateful for. P and A found their own summer rhythms -- P is outside as much as possible, A is reading or in her room creating something. All three of them ended up using the portable fans we got for their rooms more than anything else. Arkansas summers demand it.

For Me

Summer is the only time I truly decompress from the school year. I read more, I sleep later, I reorganize things that have been bugging me all year. This summer I finally tackled our linen closet (it's beautiful now, you're welcome future house guests) and reorganized the pantry. Storage bins, drawer organizers, and label makers are my version of retail therapy and Walmart has everything I need.

I also read four books this summer which felt like a personal triumph. More on those in a future post.

The Bottom Line

You don't need a big vacation or a fancy backyard to have a great summer. You need people you love and a home that feels good to be in. We've got both, and most of what makes our home feel that way came from Walmart without breaking the bank.

More summer and back-to-school posts coming soon. Thanks for reading.

-- Christin Marie
