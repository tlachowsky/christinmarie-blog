What I Read This Winter (and What's Next on My Nightstand)

Affiliate disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through my links I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. See my full disclosure policy.

January is my favorite reading month. The holidays are over, the house is quiet, the kids are back in school, and there is absolutely no reason to feel guilty about sitting down with a book for a few hours. This is my time. I protect it.

I've been a reader my whole life -- it's part of why I became a teacher, honestly. Books were always a safe place for me and I want that for my students too. There are books in my classroom that I bought myself specifically because I wanted certain kids to have access to certain stories. That's a whole other post.

Here's what I read this winter and what I thought.

What I Finished

I read three books in January alone which felt like a personal record given the school year was in full swing. I'm not going to claim I read them all during leisure time -- some of that was a book in my hand while things simmered on the stove, and some of it was staying up past when I should have. Worth it.

I gravitate toward thrillers in winter. Something about the cold and the dark makes a good suspenseful read feel exactly right. I also read one romance novel that a colleague recommended and I'm not going to pretend I didn't love it.

What's on the Nightstand Now

My nightstand stack is a legitimate source of stress for my husband. I currently have four books in various states of being read, which he finds baffling and I find completely reasonable. You need options. Different books for different moods. This is not complicated.

Making the Reading Nook Actually Work

I have a dedicated reading spot in our house -- a chair in the corner of our bedroom with a side table, a lamp, and a blanket situation that has gotten slightly out of hand but that I fully endorse. Creating a space that's specifically for reading makes you actually use it. It signals to your brain that this is what we do here.

Everything in my reading nook came from Walmart. The lamp was a find -- warm light, good height, the exact right amount of glow for evening reading. The throw blanket is soft enough that Carmy tries to steal it regularly, which I take as a quality endorsement. The side table holds my book, my tea, and whatever I'm currently using as a bookmark (varies wildly).

Getting the Kids to Read

A is a reader like me. She goes through books fast and has good taste. P will read if I find exactly the right book for him, which takes some work but is very satisfying when it happens. E reads when she has time and has always had a book or two going through high school. I'm proud of all of them for this.

We have bookshelves in the living room that are available to everyone. Keeping books visible and accessible matters. Kids read what's around them.

What's Coming

I'm starting a little book recommendation section on this blog because people ask me what I'm reading more than almost anything else. More on that soon.

Happy reading, everyone.

-- Christin Marie
