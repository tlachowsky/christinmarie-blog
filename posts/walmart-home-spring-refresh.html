My Spring Home Refresh Haul (All Walmart, All Under $200 Total)

Every spring I get the urge to refresh something in the house. Not a full renovation -- I am not that person and our budget is not that budget. More like a thoughtful refresh. New throw pillows. A different rug. Moving some things around and donating what we've outgrown. It costs relatively little and makes the house feel genuinely different.

This year I set a hard limit of $200 for the whole thing. Here's what I got, where it went, and whether it was worth it.

Living Room

New throw pillows. I do this almost every spring -- it's the fastest way to change the feel of a room. Swapped out the darker wintery tones for lighter colors and the living room immediately felt like a different season. Four throw pillow covers from Walmart's home section. Done. Under $30 total.

A new throw blanket in a lighter weight. The heavy fleece blankets of winter get folded away and something lighter and brighter comes out. Carmy was immediately on this new blanket and I've accepted that it's now also his.

Kitchen

New hand towels and a new dish drying mat. These are the things that get dingy over time and you stop noticing until you replace them and suddenly your kitchen looks fresh. This is a very low cost refresh that I recommend to everyone. New dish towels in a spring color, done.

I also replaced the little rug in front of the sink that had seen better days. Walmart has a solid selection of kitchen rugs at prices that make it completely reasonable to replace them seasonally if you want to.

Bedroom

I swapped to lighter weight bedding. This is less of a purchase and more of a rotation -- we have lighter and heavier weight options that I rotate seasonally. But I did add new pillowcases this spring, which sounds minor and felt genuinely nice.

Outside

A new doormat for the front door. Ours was embarrassing. I had been walking past it for months telling myself I'd replace it and this spring I actually did. A good mat from Walmart, planted some small flowers on the front porch, and the whole entry looks cared for and welcoming. Small things matter.

The Total

Throw pillow covers: $28. Throw blanket: $18. Kitchen towels and mat: $22. Dish drying mat: $12. Kitchen rug: $19. Bedroom pillowcases: $14. Doormat: $16. Porch plants: $24. Total: $153.

Under $200 with money to spare. The house feels like spring. That's the whole assignment.

-- Christin Marie
