The Home Organization Products I Come Back to Every Single Year

I need my house to be organized. This is not a preference -- it is a personality trait, possibly a character flaw, definitely something my family has accepted about me. A cluttered space makes my brain feel cluttered, and a cluttered brain makes me a less functional person, mother, and teacher. So the organization happens.

What I have learned over years of organizing a house with three kids, a dog, and a full-time job: you don't need to spend a lot of money to have an organized home. You need the right containers, a consistent system, and the willingness to actually maintain it (the hardest part, honestly).

Almost everything I use comes from Walmart. I have tried the expensive organization stores. I have tried the trendy bins everyone posts about on social media. I keep coming back to Walmart because the prices are reasonable, the quality holds up, and I can replace things without feeling like I need to budget for it.

The Bins

Clear bins. Always clear. I want to see what's in something without opening it. I have clear bins in my pantry, my kids' bathroom cabinets, the linen closet, the garage -- everywhere. Walmart's storage section carries exactly what I need in multiple sizes and they stack properly, which matters more than people realize until they have a stack that doesn't work.

The Drawer Organizers

I have drawer organizers in every drawer in this house. Kitchen junk drawer (which is now the kitchen organized drawer), bathroom drawers, my desk drawers. Once a drawer has an organizer in it, it stays organized almost on its own because everything has a place to go back to. This is the magic of drawer organizers and I will die on this hill.

The Labels

I use a label maker. I know this sounds intense. It is a little intense. But when a label is on a bin, everyone in the house knows where things go, and more importantly, where to find things. "Mom where is the--" questions have decreased significantly since the labels arrived. Worth every penny.

The Closet Organizers

We tackled our linen closet this past summer and it was transformative. Shelf dividers, small bins for toiletries, a dedicated section for each person's towels and extra sheets. It looks like a magazine photo in there now and I open it just to look at it sometimes. My family thinks this is funny. I don't care.

Carmy's Corner

Even Carmy's stuff is organized. His leashes, treats, grooming supplies -- all in a basket near the back door. He does not care about this. I care about this. That's enough.

The Truth About Organization

The stuff is just stuff. The real work is the system behind it and getting everyone in the house to use it. With teenagers that requires some ongoing negotiation. But a well-organized home is worth the effort and you don't have to spend a fortune to get there. Walmart makes sure of that.

I'll be sharing more specific room-by-room organization posts this fall. Stay tuned.

-- Christin Marie
