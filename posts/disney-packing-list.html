Our Disney Packing List: What We Actually Use vs. What We Regret Bringing

We are a Disney family. This is not a casual statement. We have been multiple times, we plan trips the way some people plan weddings, and I have opinions about Disney packing that I have earned through actual experience -- including the time we forgot sunscreen entirely and the time P decided to wear brand new shoes on day one and we spent an afternoon finding blister bandages in the park. Lessons were learned.

I am the packer in this family. I have a list. It has been refined over many trips. I'm sharing it here because I get asked about Disney prep constantly and I might as well put it all in one place.

The Non-Negotiables

Sunscreen. Bring it from home. The sunscreen at Disney is wildly overpriced and you will use more than you think. We bring a big bottle per person per day in a Florida summer and I have never once thought "we brought too much sunscreen." Walmart is the move here -- big bottles, good prices, same product you'd pay three times as much for in the park.

A small day bag or backpack for each person. Not huge. Just enough for water bottle, sunscreen reapplication, a light layer for when you go inside and freeze, phone charger and battery pack, and snacks. Everyone carries their own. This is the system and it works.

Comfortable shoes that have been broken in. This cannot be stressed enough. P's blister incident is a cautionary tale I revisit every single trip. Whatever shoes you're bringing, wear them for a week before you go. Every single day. Break them in fully. Disney is a walking trip and your feet will log serious miles.

A portable phone charger. Battery pack. Whatever you want to call it. Your phone will die faster than you expect with GPS, photos, and whatever apps you're using. Having a fully charged backup in your bag is non-negotiable.

The Things That Actually Make the Trip Better

A small first aid kit with blister bandages specifically. See above. Also ibuprofen because theme parks are physically demanding and you will feel it.

Ponchos. Compact, cheap, available at Walmart for a fraction of what they charge in the park. Florida will rain at some point. Usually in the afternoon. You will be glad you have a poncho.

Snacks from home. We pack snacks every day and bring them in. A small soft cooler fits easily in a day bag and keeps things cool. Disney allows outside food and this is a significant money saver over the course of a multi-day trip.

Matching family shirts if that's your thing. We do this. The teenagers have accepted it. I buy ours from Walmart and they always turn out great -- comfortable, look good in photos, and nobody paid $40 per shirt for them.

What We've Stopped Bringing

Autograph books. We did these when the kids were small. They've outgrown it and honestly the character meet times are better used for photos now.

Too many "just in case" items. I used to overpack out of anxiety. A light bag is actually better. You'll move faster and your shoulders will thank you at the end of day three.

The Money Saving Part

Disney is expensive. The tickets are expensive, the hotels are expensive, the food in the park is expensive. You cannot control most of that. What you can control is what you bring from home -- and buying that stuff at Walmart before you leave means you're not paying park prices for things you could have had for a fraction of the cost.

We spend real money on the experience and save everywhere we possibly can on the supplies. That's the Disney budget philosophy in this house and it has served us well.
